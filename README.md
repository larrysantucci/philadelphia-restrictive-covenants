# Philadelphia Restrictive Covenants Dataset

A spatial dataset documenting racially restrictive covenants in 20th-century Philadelphia, constructed from historical deed records at the Philadelphia Department of Records.

> The views expressed here are solely those of the author and do not necessarily reflect the views of the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia or the Federal Reserve System.

---

## About the Data

Racially restrictive covenants were clauses in property deeds that prohibited the sale, lease, or occupation of homes by people of specific racial or ethnic groups. Though declared unenforceable by the U.S. Supreme Court in *Shelley v. Kraemer* (1948), these covenants remained in deeds and continued to shape residential segregation patterns for decades.

This dataset maps the geographic extent of racially restrictive covenants in Philadelphia, identifying specific properties and neighborhoods where these restrictions were recorded. The data was constructed through systematic examination of historical deed records.
